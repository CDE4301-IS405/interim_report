1.1 Sub-section 1 IAQ (example only)

Air quality is fundamental to human health and well-being. The air we breathe influences a wide range of physiological functions, including respiratory, cardiovascular, and cognitive systems (World Health Organisation, n.d.). Traditionally, the focus has been on outdoor air pollution, since its effects are visible through smog, haze, and poor air indices, and it has been established as a major global health risk. Indoor air quality remains a neglected aspect of environmental health (Dcarrick, 2025). Unlike outdoor air pollution, which is visible through haze or measurable by public indices, indoor pollutants are often invisible and odorless, making them harder to detect and easier to overlook.

6.1. Phase 4: Microbe Selection

The selection of microbes for this experiment was based on several key criteria to ensure safety, practicality, and effectiveness in phytoremediation applications.

Selection Criteria Ideal Case Remarks
Availability Easily Available in Singapore Microbes must be easily accessible and feasible to procure within Singapore, ensuring practicality for both the experimental setup and future applications.
Biosafety Risk Low Low biosafety risk ensures that handling is safe even in non-specialised lab environments, minimising potential health or environmental risks. The biosafety risk is determined through reading the Safety Data Sheet, ensuring that no special precautions are required (such as in a fume hood)
Environmental Compatibility Temperature where microbes thrive 20 to 25°C 20 to 25°C corresponds to the temperatures of most indoor enclosed spaces where air-conditioning is typically used in Singapore.
pH tolerance pH= 6 to 7 pH range of 6 to 7 corresponds to the pH of LECA balls in the plant-microbe system. This ensures microbes can remain effective in removing VOCs in the system they are in.
